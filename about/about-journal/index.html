About the Journal

The International Journal of Advanced Research and Interdisciplinary Scientific Endeavours (abbreviated as IJARISE) is an international, multidisciplinary, and open access journal that monthly reports emerging scientific papers and advanced research across multidisciplinary disciplines. Additionally, the journal encourages research paper publication from sophomores to academic professionals, providing a pioneering platform for original research and journal papers.

In contrast to conventional publishers and scopus indexed journals, we remain distinct in reporting concepts, hypotheses, early-stage research, theses, proposals, and negative results, thereby enriching substantial content through rewriting journal papers for successful publication. In addition to this, the prime scope of this journal is to produce extraordinary scientific papers to revolutionize modern research and to bridge the scientific gap between current and exemplary research. Hence, this journal seems to be the best of all journals to publish paper, and are highly endorsed by emeritus professors and emerging scholars. The International Journal of Advanced Research and Interdisciplinary Scientific Endeavours is endorsed by its own editorial board members of Acceleron Aerospace Sciences Private Limited.

Moreover, all scientific papers published in this journal are licensed under CC BY 4.0 International, wherein the journal retains the copyright of all published content, and all other rights are retained by the author.

Furthermore, we prioritize the significance and privacy of all information provided to us. We do not support or encourage the sharing of personal or any sort of sensitive data with third parties for exploitation. All information is secured and protected with data protection and privacy regulations associated with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Finally, all scientific papers reported in this journal are digitally preserved under LOCKSS and CLOCKSS services and archived in several repositories such as PKP Preservation Network, Portico, OpenAIRE, and Scholar Archives.
